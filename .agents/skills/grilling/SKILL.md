---
name: grilling
description: Interview the researcher relentlessly about a research plan, study design, method, dataset, claim, interpretation, paper argument, or output. Use when the researcher wants to stress-test research before analysis or writing, or uses any grill trigger phrases.
---

Interview the researcher relentlessly about every aspect of the research plan until you reach a shared understanding. Walk down each branch of the research design tree, resolving dependencies between decisions one by one. For each question, provide your recommended answer or the tradeoff you see.

Ask questions one at a time, waiting for feedback on each question before continuing. Asking multiple questions at once is bewildering.

If a question can be answered by exploring the repository, data inventory, notebooks, scripts, papers, outputs, or decision records, explore those artifacts instead of asking.
